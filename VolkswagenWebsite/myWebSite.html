About Volkswagen

The Group comprises twelve brands from seven European countries: Volkswagen Passenger Cars, Audi, SEAT, ŠKODA, Bentley, Bugatti, Lamborghini, Porsche, Ducati, Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles, Scania and MAN. In addition, the Volkswagen Group offers a wide range of financial services, including dealer and customer financing, leasing, banking, insurance activities and fleet management.

1974–1983

The Golf Mk1 was sold as the 'Volkswagen Rabbit' in the United States and Canada and as the 'Volkswagen Caribe' in Mexico.

1983–1991

A Mk2-based second generation Jetta was reveiled in January 1984. There was no Mk2-based Cabriolet model. Instead, the Mk1 Cabriolet was continued over the Mk2's entire production run.

1991–1997

The Mk3 kept being sold until 1999 in the United States, Canada, parts of South America and also in Mexico as a special edition called 'Mi'.


The VW Golf had several generations, made into electric CityStromer models. The first model was in the 1970s, when VW took a standard Golf Mk1 and converted it into electric power. By the time the Golf Mk2 came into production a limited number of electric Golfs were made, using lead–acid battery packs, custom-made motor and a controller. VW continued the production of limited numbers of CityStromer electric cars with the introduction of the Golf Mk3.

With few exceptions, only left-hand drive Golfs were converted by VW into Citystromer models. These vehicles are still used today and have popularity in Europe, with only few present in Great Britain. Only two right-hand drive Mk2 CityStromers were built for the UK market and it's being assumed that only one remains today. It's owned by EV advocate and broadcaster Nikki Gordon-Bloomfield - host of Transport Evolved.
As part of the 'Fleet study in electric mobility' project, that began in 2008, VW developed 20 'Golf Variant twinDRIVE' plug-in hybrid electric vehicles. These vehicles have all-electric range of 57 km (35 mi) and the internal combustion engine provides for a total range of 900 km (560 mi). The plug-in hybrid drive of the 'Golf Variant twinDRIVE' is equipped with either an 11.2 kWh or a 13.2 kWh lithium-ion battery pack, as Volkswagen is testing packs from two vendors.

Ten vehicles equipped with batteries from the American-German manufacturer 'GAIA with cathode type nickel cobalt aluminium dioxide (NCA). Other ten vehicles are powered by lithium-ion batteries with nickel manganese cobalt (NMC) cathodes from the Korean-German joint venture SB LiMotive (Samsung and Bosch). These ten vehicles have been in use since early 2011. Both battery systems offer high power and energy density.
